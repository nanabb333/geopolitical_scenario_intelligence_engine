# Executive Demo Case

This demo uses an export-control scenario because it is supported by the V1 evidence base and represented in the V2 market-reaction pilot. It does not invent unsupported results.

## Question

```text
What happened historically when advanced semiconductor export controls expanded?
```

## Scenario Family

Primary scenario family: Technology Competition.

Related scenario family: Strategic Competition.

Why: The question concerns export controls, advanced semiconductors, AI chips, and US-China strategic competition.

## Historical Analogues

Relevant analogue types already represented in the project:

- BIS advanced computing and semiconductor manufacturing export controls.
- BIS updates advanced computing and semiconductor export controls.
- Japan semiconductor-equipment export-control plan.
- Entity-list actions involving semiconductor or memory-chip firms.

The analyst should compare policy mechanism, sector exposure, actor and target geography, implementation status, and source confidence.

## Market Evidence

The V2 market-reaction pilot includes historical observed rows for selected export-control and AI-chip restriction events.

Useful market evidence types:

- semiconductor sector proxy rows;
- named AI-chip exposed equity rows where defensible;
- benchmark comparison rows;
- short windows such as `t0` and `m3_p3`.

Important caveat: the V2 market layer is a static pilot dataset and is not yet integrated into the V1 workbench.

## Observed Pathways

Likely pathway frame: Restriction pathway.

Analyst review should consider:

- directly constrained firms;
- substitute beneficiaries;
- semiconductor equipment exposure;
- supply-chain rerouting;
- timing of announcement versus implementation;
- anticipation before the event date.

These are observed pathway frames, not predictions.

## Current Relevance

Current relevance should be evaluated through:

- Historical similarity: Does the scenario resemble prior export-control events?
- Structural similarity: Is it sector-wide or named-firm specific?
- Policy similarity: Is the control mechanism comparable?
- Sector exposure: Are AI chips, semiconductor equipment, memory chips, or foundries involved?
- Market evidence: Are comparable market rows available, and are proxies defensible?
- Limitations: Are there missing return rows, confounds, or unsupported parts of the question?

## Executive Memo

### Bottom Line

The strongest historical frame for this question is Technology Competition, specifically export controls and AI-chip restrictions. The evidence base can support historical analogue review and limited market-reaction context where pilot market rows exist.

### Evidence to Use

- V1 historical analogue workflow for semiconductor export-control and entity-list cases.
- V2 US-China pilot events for broader strategic competition context.
- V2 market-reaction pilot rows for selected export-control and AI-chip restriction events.

### Caveats

- The V2 market layer is not fully integrated into the V1 workflow.
- Market rows are historical observations only.
- Proxy choices are imperfect and must be disclosed.
- The memo should not include forecasts, probabilities, expected returns, price targets, trading signals, or investment advice.

### Recommended Analyst Output

A concise Markdown memo explaining the scenario family, closest analogues, available market evidence, restriction pathway, current relevance, and limitations.
