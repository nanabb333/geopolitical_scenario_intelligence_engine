# Intelligence Playbooks

These playbooks define reusable workflows for common geopolitical risk questions. They guide analyst use of the existing deterministic workflow; they do not add forecasting, probabilities, investment advice, or new product logic.

## Playbook 1: Export Control Escalation

- Trigger: New or expanded export controls on technology, equipment, chips, software, or sensitive components.
- Questions to ask:
  - What mechanism is being restricted?
  - Is the event firm-specific or sector-wide?
  - Which prior export-control events are closest?
  - Is market evidence available for comparable events?
- Relevant scenario families: Technology Competition, Strategic Competition.
- Historical analogue approach: Prioritize export-control and AI-chip restriction analogues with similar policy mechanism and sector exposure.
- Market evidence approach: Review sector proxies first, then named firms where exposure is defensible.
- Current relevance approach: Compare policy similarity, target geography, sector exposure, and implementation status.
- Executive memo focus: Restriction pathway, direct versus indirect exposure, market evidence caveats.

## Playbook 2: AI Chip Restriction

- Trigger: New controls affecting AI accelerators, advanced computing, HBM, cloud access, or chip manufacturing capability.
- Questions to ask:
  - Is the scenario about chips, cloud, equipment, or manufacturing capability?
  - Are named firms exposed?
  - Are substitute beneficiaries plausible?
  - Which prior AI-chip events have market rows?
- Relevant scenario families: Technology Competition, Strategic Competition.
- Historical analogue approach: Retrieve advanced computing, AI chip, and semiconductor export-control events.
- Market evidence approach: Use named firms only when exposure is clear; otherwise use semiconductor or technology proxies.
- Current relevance approach: Separate technical rule similarity from broad strategic competition framing.
- Executive memo focus: Technology restriction mechanism, named-firm caveats, proxy limitations.

## Playbook 3: Industrial Policy and Subsidy Support

- Trigger: Subsidy package, industrial-policy law, funding award, tax credit, or state-backed manufacturing support.
- Questions to ask:
  - Is the event broad legislation or firm-specific award?
  - Was the support anticipated?
  - Who is the direct beneficiary?
  - Does market evidence exist for comparable support events?
- Relevant scenario families: Industrial Policy, Supply Chain Risk.
- Historical analogue approach: Retrieve subsidy, fab investment, CHIPS Act, and state-support analogues.
- Market evidence approach: Use named beneficiaries when available; otherwise sector proxies.
- Current relevance approach: Compare support materiality, credibility, directness, and anticipation.
- Executive memo focus: State-support pathway, anticipation caveat, beneficiary visibility.

## Playbook 4: Economic Security and Investment Screening

- Trigger: Outbound investment rule, procurement restriction, national-security review, or sensitive-technology screening action.
- Questions to ask:
  - Is this a framework announcement or final rule?
  - Which sectors are covered?
  - Is the action firm-specific or sector-wide?
  - What prior investment-screening or procurement restrictions are comparable?
- Relevant scenario families: Economic Security, Strategic Competition.
- Historical analogue approach: Compare investment-screening, procurement restriction, and entity-list events.
- Market evidence approach: Use broad technology proxies unless named firms are directly identified.
- Current relevance approach: Separate policy framework from implementation and enforcement.
- Executive memo focus: Economic security rationale, implementation uncertainty, data limitations.

## Playbook 5: Critical Minerals and Resource Controls

- Trigger: Export controls, processing restrictions, stockpile decisions, or strategic-materials policy.
- Questions to ask:
  - Which minerals or inputs are restricted?
  - What downstream sectors are exposed?
  - Is a direct commodity proxy available?
  - How reliable is the proxy mapping?
- Relevant scenario families: Resource Competition, Supply Chain Risk.
- Historical analogue approach: Retrieve critical-mineral and strategic-input restriction events.
- Market evidence approach: Use materials, mining, battery, or sector proxies with strong caveats.
- Current relevance approach: Compare input role, affected sectors, and source confidence.
- Executive memo focus: Resource-competition mechanism, proxy limitations, missing-data caveats.

## Playbook 6: Supply Chain Relocation

- Trigger: Reshoring, friend-shoring, strategic capacity investment, or state-backed relocation announcement.
- Questions to ask:
  - Is the event a relocation, capacity expansion, or subsidy?
  - Is a named beneficiary identified?
  - Is the supply-chain channel direct or indirect?
  - What similar state-support or relocation events exist?
- Relevant scenario families: Supply Chain Risk, Industrial Policy.
- Historical analogue approach: Retrieve fab investment, supply-chain relocation, and state-support cases.
- Market evidence approach: Use named beneficiaries or sector proxies; document regional exposure.
- Current relevance approach: Compare directness, implementation status, and capacity relevance.
- Executive memo focus: Reallocation pathway, beneficiary visibility, support materiality.

## Playbook 7: Executive Briefing

- Trigger: Leadership asks for a short evidence-backed note on a geopolitical risk scenario.
- Questions to ask:
  - What is the scenario family?
  - Which analogues are closest?
  - What market evidence exists?
  - What is unsupported by current data?
- Relevant scenario families: All.
- Historical analogue approach: Use the strongest matching mechanism and sector, not superficial topic similarity.
- Market evidence approach: Include only observed historical rows and disclose gaps.
- Current relevance approach: Separate historical evidence from current uncertainty.
- Executive memo focus: Bottom line, evidence, caveats, and recommended analyst follow-up without advice.
