# Analyst Use Cases

These use cases describe how analysts would use the system as a deterministic decision-support workflow. They do not require forecasts, probabilities, expected returns, investment advice, LLMs, or external APIs.

## 1. Export Control Escalation Analysis

- Analyst objective: Compare a new export-control scenario with prior technology restriction events.
- Typical question: What happened when advanced semiconductor export controls expanded?
- Workflow path: Question -> Technology Competition -> Historical Analogues -> Market Evidence -> Observed Pathways -> Executive Memo.
- Output generated: Workbench memo with retrieved analogues, restriction pathway framing, market evidence where available, and caveats.
- Business value: Speeds first-pass risk review and standardizes export-control discussion.

## 2. AI Chip Restriction Assessment

- Analyst objective: Understand historical analogues for restrictions on AI accelerators or advanced computing.
- Typical question: Which prior AI chip restrictions are most comparable to this scenario?
- Workflow path: Question -> Technology Competition -> AI Chip Restrictions -> Market Evidence -> Current Relevance.
- Output generated: Scenario brief with relevant analogues and historical market-reaction rows when available.
- Business value: Helps analysts frame high-profile technology risk without making price predictions.

## 3. Strategic Investment Screening Review

- Analyst objective: Assess prior investment-screening actions and their market context.
- Typical question: How have outbound investment restrictions affected sensitive technology sectors historically?
- Workflow path: Question -> Economic Security -> Historical Analogues -> Current Relevance -> Executive Memo.
- Output generated: Memo focused on policy similarity, implementation status, and sector exposure.
- Business value: Supports policy-risk review for strategy, legal, or market intelligence teams.

## 4. Supply-Chain Relocation Analysis

- Analyst objective: Compare current relocation or friend-shoring scenarios with prior strategic capacity events.
- Typical question: What historical analogues exist for state-backed semiconductor supply-chain relocation?
- Workflow path: Question -> Supply Chain Risk -> Historical Analogues -> Market Evidence -> Observed Pathways.
- Output generated: Evidence note on relocation analogues, support mechanisms, and proxy limitations.
- Business value: Clarifies direct beneficiary versus broader sector exposure.

## 5. Industrial Policy Comparison

- Analyst objective: Compare subsidy or industrial-policy announcements across historical cases.
- Typical question: How have semiconductor subsidy programs affected market proxies historically?
- Workflow path: Question -> Industrial Policy -> Historical Analogues -> Market Evidence -> Executive Memo.
- Output generated: Analyst workbench report with state-support pathway and anticipation caveats.
- Business value: Makes industrial-policy analysis repeatable and committee-ready.

## 6. Critical-Mineral Restriction Assessment

- Analyst objective: Understand historical evidence around strategic input controls.
- Typical question: What happened after critical-mineral export restrictions?
- Workflow path: Question -> Resource Competition -> Historical Analogues -> Market Evidence -> Current Relevance.
- Output generated: Brief identifying resource-competition analogues, proxy limitations, and missing data boundaries.
- Business value: Expands risk review beyond semiconductors while keeping evidence caveats visible.

## 7. Technology Competition Monitoring

- Analyst objective: Maintain a structured view of technology-policy escalation mechanisms.
- Typical question: Which recent events fit technology competition rather than industrial policy?
- Workflow path: Question -> Scenario Family -> Historical Analogues -> Observed Pathways.
- Output generated: Scenario-family classification note and analogue shortlist.
- Business value: Helps teams organize recurring technology-risk questions consistently.

## 8. Sanctions Impact Review

- Analyst objective: Compare named-target restrictions with broader export-control analogues.
- Typical question: How do entity-list actions differ from export-control rules in historical evidence?
- Workflow path: Question -> Economic Security -> Historical Analogues -> Market Evidence -> Memo Caveats.
- Output generated: Comparison brief separating named-company exposure from sector-wide policy effects.
- Business value: Reduces confusion between sanctions, entity lists, procurement restrictions, and export controls.

## 9. Cross-Case Policy Comparison

- Analyst objective: Compare multiple policy mechanisms across the US-China strategic competition dataset.
- Typical question: Is this scenario closer to export controls, investment screening, or industrial policy?
- Workflow path: Question -> Strategic Competition -> Scenario Family Comparison -> Historical Analogues -> Current Relevance.
- Output generated: Evidence matrix or memo narrative explaining policy similarity and differences.
- Business value: Improves strategic clarity before executive discussion.

## 10. Executive Briefing Preparation

- Analyst objective: Prepare a concise evidence-backed briefing for leadership.
- Typical question: What should a risk committee know about this technology restriction scenario?
- Workflow path: Question -> Scenario Family -> Analogues -> Market Evidence -> Pathways -> Executive Memo.
- Output generated: Markdown memo with bottom line, analogue evidence, market evidence caveats, and limitations.
- Business value: Converts analyst research into a repeatable executive communication artifact.

## 11. Dataset Coverage Review

- Analyst objective: Determine whether a question is currently supported by available data.
- Typical question: Can the system support a Taiwan blockade question today?
- Workflow path: Question -> Supported Boundary Review -> Scenario Family -> Coverage Caveat.
- Output generated: Scope note identifying supported, partially supported, or unsupported framing.
- Business value: Prevents overclaiming and protects credibility.

## 12. Market Evidence Triage

- Analyst objective: Identify whether retrieved analogues have market reaction data.
- Typical question: Which analogues have observed event-window market evidence?
- Workflow path: Question -> Historical Analogues -> Market Evidence Availability -> Data Quality Review.
- Output generated: Market evidence summary with missing-row disclosure and proxy caveats.
- Business value: Separates evidence-backed comparisons from analogue-only comparisons.
