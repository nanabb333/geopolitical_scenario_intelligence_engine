# Scenario Taxonomy

This taxonomy organizes analyst questions into scenario families before historical analogue retrieval. It is a product layer for question framing, not a new model, forecast, or scoring system.

## Technology Competition

### Definition

Technology competition scenarios involve state action that restricts, supports, or redirects access to strategic technologies such as semiconductors, AI chips, cloud infrastructure, equipment, and advanced computing.

### Subcategories

- Export controls
- AI chip restrictions
- Entity-list actions
- Cloud or compute access restrictions
- Technology-transfer limits

### Example Historical Events

- BIS advanced computing and semiconductor manufacturing export controls.
- BIS updates advanced computing and semiconductor export controls.
- YMTC and other Chinese entities added to the Entity List.

### Typical Analyst Questions

- What happened when export controls expanded?
- Which technology sectors were most exposed?
- How did named firms compare with sector proxies?
- What restrictions were most similar to the current scenario?

### Market Relevance

Technology competition can affect semiconductor firms, AI infrastructure, cloud providers, equipment suppliers, memory-chip producers, and substitute beneficiaries.

## Industrial Policy

### Definition

Industrial policy scenarios involve state funding, tax incentives, manufacturing support, strategic capacity programs, or national development plans aimed at building domestic capabilities.

### Subcategories

- Semiconductor subsidies
- Strategic manufacturing legislation
- State-backed investment funds
- Fab support
- Domestic capacity programs

### Example Historical Events

- CHIPS and Science Act signed.
- TSMC Arizona CHIPS preliminary terms announced.
- China semiconductor Big Fund III registered.

### Typical Analyst Questions

- How have subsidy programs affected markets historically?
- Which firms were direct beneficiaries?
- Were policy actions anticipated before announcement?
- What pathway followed large state-support announcements?

### Market Relevance

Industrial policy can affect named beneficiaries, equipment suppliers, regional manufacturing exposure, semiconductor indexes, and long-term capacity expectations.

## Economic Security

### Definition

Economic security scenarios involve controls on investment, procurement, access to capital, or sensitive economic relationships justified by national security or strategic-risk concerns.

### Subcategories

- Outbound investment screening
- Procurement restrictions
- National-security review
- Entity-list actions
- Technology-sector restrictions

### Example Historical Events

- US executive order on outbound investment in sensitive technologies.
- US Treasury finalizes outbound investment rule for China-related technologies.
- China restricts Micron products in critical information infrastructure procurement.

### Typical Analyst Questions

- How have investment restrictions changed investor behavior historically?
- Which sectors were most exposed to national-security screening?
- How did procurement restrictions affect named firms?
- What analogues exist for economic security controls?

### Market Relevance

Economic security actions can affect technology firms, cross-border capital flows, supplier relationships, procurement exposure, and regional equity risk.

## Resource Competition

### Definition

Resource competition scenarios involve state control, restriction, or strategic support for materials and inputs important to technology, defense, energy, or industrial supply chains.

### Subcategories

- Critical-mineral export controls
- Rare-earth processing restrictions
- Strategic stockpiles
- Mineral processing subsidies
- Input security policies

### Example Historical Events

- China announces gallium and germanium export controls.
- China announces graphite export controls.
- China restricts export of rare-earth processing technology.

### Typical Analyst Questions

- What happened after critical-mineral restrictions?
- Which sectors had the clearest exposure?
- How should analysts compare mineral controls with technology controls?
- What market proxies are appropriate when direct exposure is hard to isolate?

### Market Relevance

Resource competition can affect materials firms, semiconductor inputs, battery supply chains, defense suppliers, clean-energy firms, and broad commodity-linked proxies.

## Supply Chain Risk

### Definition

Supply chain risk scenarios involve geopolitical actions that relocate, disrupt, reroute, or reprice strategic supply chains.

### Subcategories

- Supply-chain relocation
- Friend-shoring
- Strategic fab relocation
- Maritime disruption
- Supplier concentration risk

### Example Historical Events

- TSMC Arizona CHIPS preliminary terms announced.
- Japan announces semiconductor-equipment export-control plan.
- Taiwan-related tension affecting semiconductor supply-chain risk.

### Typical Analyst Questions

- What patterns emerge following major supply-chain disruptions?
- Which analogues show relocation or substitution pathways?
- How should analysts distinguish direct beneficiaries from substitute beneficiaries?
- What market proxies best represent supply-chain exposure?

### Market Relevance

Supply chain risk can affect foundries, equipment suppliers, logistics firms, regional indexes, commodity inputs, and firms with concentrated production exposure.

## Strategic Competition

### Definition

Strategic competition scenarios involve state actions where economic, technological, military, or resource policy is used as part of broader geopolitical rivalry.

### Subcategories

- US-China strategic competition
- Taiwan-related tension
- Allied technology alignment
- Retaliation and countermeasures
- Strategic investment screening

### Example Historical Events

- US-China export-control updates.
- China-side procurement restrictions.
- Taiwan-related military drills as one subcategory within broader strategic competition.

### Typical Analyst Questions

- Which historical analogues best match the current strategic-competition mechanism?
- Is the current scenario closer to industrial policy, restriction, retaliation, or supply-chain risk?
- What market evidence exists from similar historical events?
- What caveats should an executive memo include?

### Market Relevance

Strategic competition can affect technology, semiconductors, critical minerals, industrials, defense, logistics, regional markets, and broad risk sentiment.
