# Analyst Personas

## Risk Analyst

- Goals: Identify relevant historical analogues, flag exposure channels, and prepare risk committee evidence.
- Pain points: Fragmented sources, inconsistent scenario framing, pressure to respond quickly.
- Typical questions:
  - What historical events resemble this risk scenario?
  - Which sectors were exposed in prior cases?
  - What caveats should be disclosed?
- Why this system helps: Provides a repeatable question-to-evidence workflow with boundaries against forecasts and investment advice.

## Strategy Analyst

- Goals: Understand how geopolitical policy changes affect strategic planning and competitive positioning.
- Pain points: Difficulty connecting policy events to business implications without overclaiming.
- Typical questions:
  - Is this event closer to industrial policy or restriction?
  - Which historical analogues show supply-chain relocation?
  - What strategic pathways should be monitored?
- Why this system helps: Organizes scenarios into families and converts analogues into executive-ready context.

## Market Intelligence Analyst

- Goals: Track market-relevant geopolitical events and summarize observed historical market evidence.
- Pain points: Need to distinguish descriptive market evidence from speculative interpretation.
- Typical questions:
  - Which analogues have market reaction rows?
  - What proxies were used?
  - What data limitations apply?
- Why this system helps: Combines historical analogues with documented market evidence and proxy caveats.

## Policy Analyst

- Goals: Compare policy mechanisms, implementation status, and precedent across geopolitical events.
- Pain points: Similar-sounding policies often differ in scope, timing, and enforcement.
- Typical questions:
  - Is this an export control, sanction, procurement restriction, or investment screen?
  - What prior policy mechanisms are comparable?
  - What source and coding caveats matter?
- Why this system helps: Separates scenario families, policy similarity, and current relevance without making market predictions.

## Corporate Planning Analyst

- Goals: Translate geopolitical risk into planning context for supply chains, manufacturing, and strategic investment.
- Pain points: Broad geopolitical narratives are hard to convert into structured business planning inputs.
- Typical questions:
  - What historical events involved supply-chain relocation?
  - Which sectors were affected by strategic input controls?
  - What evidence can support planning assumptions?
- Why this system helps: Produces structured briefs with analogues, pathways, and limitations for planning discussions.
