# Event Dictionary

This dictionary defines standardized V2 event categories for future geopolitical dataset expansion. It is intended to make coding consistent across domains while preserving the current deterministic workflow architecture.

## US-China Strategic Competition Pilot Dataset

### Definition

A V2 pilot dataset umbrella for events that materially reflect strategic competition between the United States and China through technology controls, sanctions, industrial policy, supply chains, critical minerals, investment screening, AI infrastructure, or related geopolitical tension.

This is an expansion frame, not a claim that the current dataset already covers all listed mechanisms.

### Inclusion Criteria

- Export controls or technology-transfer restrictions involving U.S.-China competition.
- Sanctions, entity-list actions, procurement bans, or investment restrictions.
- AI chip restrictions, compute controls, or AI infrastructure competition.
- Semiconductor industrial policy linked to strategic competition.
- Critical-minerals restrictions, stockpiling, or processing-policy actions.
- Supply-chain relocation or friend-shoring actions linked to U.S.-China risk.
- Strategic investment screening or national-security review actions.
- Taiwan-related tension when it affects U.S.-China strategic competition, technology supply chains, shipping, or market risk.

### Exclusion Criteria

- Taiwan-related events with no U.S.-China strategic-competition relevance.
- General bilateral diplomacy without market, policy, security, or supply-chain mechanism.
- Routine trade disputes without strategic competition, security, technology, or supply-chain relevance.
- Events that require forecasting, probability estimates, or investment recommendations to interpret.

### Example Events

- Advanced chip export-control update.
- Entity-list action against a technology firm.
- Strategic investment screening decision.
- Critical-minerals export restriction.
- Supply-chain relocation policy.
- Taiwan-related military or diplomatic tension affecting technology supply-chain risk.

## Semiconductor Policy

### Definition

Government policy, funding, regulation, or strategic action intended to support, restrict, or reshape semiconductor production, supply chains, or competitiveness.

### Inclusion Criteria

- Semiconductor subsidy awards.
- CHIPS Act or equivalent industrial-policy actions.
- Fab investment support.
- Semiconductor procurement or capacity policy.

### Exclusion Criteria

- Broad technology policy without a semiconductor link.
- Company earnings or capital expenditure announcements without government policy relevance.

### Example Events

- Semiconductor subsidy award.
- State-backed fab investment.
- Industrial-policy legislation targeting chip manufacturing.

## Export Controls

### Definition

Government restrictions on export, transfer, licensing, or access to sensitive goods, software, equipment, or technology.

### Inclusion Criteria

- Export-license requirements.
- Advanced chip restrictions.
- Semiconductor-equipment controls.
- Allied export-control alignment.

### Exclusion Criteria

- General trade disputes without strategic technology controls.
- Tariffs without security or technology-transfer rationale.

### Example Events

- New advanced AI-chip export-control rule.
- Semiconductor-equipment export restrictions.
- License requirement for controlled technology.

## Sanctions

### Definition

Legal or administrative restrictions imposed on firms, individuals, governments, sectors, or financial flows for geopolitical, security, or policy reasons.

### Inclusion Criteria

- Entity-list actions.
- Financial sanctions.
- Procurement bans.
- Secondary sanctions.
- Sectoral sanctions.

### Exclusion Criteria

- Export controls that do not sanction a target actor.
- Nonbinding political criticism without legal restriction.

### Example Events

- Entity-list designation.
- Sectoral sanctions on a strategic industry.
- Procurement ban against named firms.

## Sanctions / Entity List

### Definition

Entity-list, blacklist, procurement-ban, or sanctions-style actions that restrict named firms, organizations, or sectors in a US-China strategic competition context.

### Inclusion Criteria

- Commerce Entity List additions.
- Procurement restrictions against named firms.
- Sanctions or blacklist actions with technology, security, or supply-chain relevance.

### Exclusion Criteria

- Broad export-control rules without named targets.
- General diplomatic statements without legal or procurement consequence.

### Example Events

- Addition of a Chinese technology firm to the Entity List.
- Procurement restriction against a named semiconductor company.
- Sectoral restriction affecting strategic technology access.

## AI Chip Restrictions

### Definition

Controls, license requirements, or policy actions that restrict access to advanced AI accelerators, high-performance computing chips, or related AI-chip manufacturing capability.

### Inclusion Criteria

- Advanced AI-chip export restrictions.
- High-bandwidth memory or accelerator controls.
- Restrictions on AI-chip manufacturing equipment or software.
- Controls aimed at preventing military or strategic AI capability transfer.

### Exclusion Criteria

- General AI regulation without compute or chip infrastructure relevance.
- Semiconductor policy without AI-chip or advanced-computing connection.

### Example Events

- Updated AI accelerator export controls.
- HBM restrictions tied to AI-chip production.
- License rules for advanced computing items.

## Industrial Policy

### Definition

Government funding, legislation, tax incentives, or strategic programs designed to build domestic industrial capacity in strategically important sectors.

### Inclusion Criteria

- Semiconductor subsidies.
- Strategic manufacturing legislation.
- State-backed technology funds.
- Industrial-capacity support tied to national security or strategic competition.

### Exclusion Criteria

- Company-only capital expenditure with no policy support.
- Routine economic policy without strategic sector targeting.

### Example Events

- CHIPS Act enactment.
- National semiconductor fund launch.
- Strategic manufacturing subsidy program.

## Strategic Investment Screening

### Definition

Rules, executive actions, or review mechanisms that screen, restrict, or require notification of cross-border investment in strategic sectors.

### Inclusion Criteria

- Outbound investment restrictions.
- National-security investment review rules.
- Restrictions on investment in sensitive technology sectors.

### Exclusion Criteria

- Routine merger review without national-security rationale.
- Capital-market movements without policy action.

### Example Events

- Executive order on outbound investment in sensitive technologies.
- Final rule restricting investment in advanced technology sectors.
- National-security review of strategic technology investment.

## Supply Chain Relocation

### Definition

Policy or firm events that materially shift strategic production, sourcing, or manufacturing capacity away from geopolitical-risk exposure.

### Inclusion Criteria

- Friend-shoring or reshoring policy actions.
- State-backed relocation of strategic manufacturing.
- Supply-chain diversification programs tied to US-China risk.

### Exclusion Criteria

- Routine facility expansion without strategic relocation rationale.
- Logistics changes without geopolitical or supply-chain-risk connection.

### Example Events

- State-backed semiconductor fab relocation.
- Supply-chain diversification away from China exposure.
- Strategic manufacturing move linked to national-security policy.

## Taiwan Tension

### Definition

Events that materially increase or decrease geopolitical risk around Taiwan, including military, diplomatic, economic, or supply-chain channels.

### Inclusion Criteria

- Taiwan Strait military exercises.
- Blockade-risk episodes.
- Airspace or maritime escalation.
- Crisis communications affecting Taiwan risk.
- Semiconductor supply-chain risk linked to Taiwan tension.

### Exclusion Criteria

- Routine diplomatic statements without risk escalation.
- Taiwan-linked semiconductor investment with no geopolitical tension component.

### Example Events

- Large military exercise near Taiwan.
- Maritime disruption warning around Taiwan Strait.
- Escalatory cross-strait policy announcement.

## Military Exercise

### Definition

Military drills or deployments that change perceived regional risk, deterrence posture, escalation risk, or supply-chain security.

### Inclusion Criteria

- Large-scale exercises near contested areas.
- Joint military drills with geopolitical signaling.
- Exercises that trigger sanctions, restrictions, or market concern.

### Exclusion Criteria

- Routine training with no geopolitical relevance.
- Defense procurement events better coded as defense industrial policy.

### Example Events

- Naval exercise near a strategic chokepoint.
- Air and sea drills following a diplomatic dispute.
- Joint exercise that raises regional risk.

## Shipping Disruption

### Definition

Events that disrupt, threaten, or materially reroute maritime shipping, logistics, or trade flows.

### Inclusion Criteria

- Strait or canal disruption.
- Port closure.
- Maritime security incident.
- Shipping rerouting due to conflict.
- Insurance or freight disruption linked to geopolitical risk.

### Exclusion Criteria

- Normal seasonal congestion.
- Company-specific logistics issue without geopolitical trigger.

### Example Events

- Maritime chokepoint disruption.
- Port closure after geopolitical escalation.
- Rerouting around conflict zone.

## Energy Security

### Definition

Events affecting energy supply, pricing channels, strategic reserves, infrastructure, or energy-related geopolitical leverage.

### Inclusion Criteria

- Oil or gas sanctions.
- Pipeline disruption.
- LNG export restriction.
- Strategic reserve release.
- Energy infrastructure attack with geopolitical relevance.

### Exclusion Criteria

- Routine energy price movement without event trigger.
- Company earnings or production updates without geopolitical relevance.

### Example Events

- Gas supply cutoff.
- Oil export sanction.
- Pipeline disruption after geopolitical escalation.

## Critical Minerals

### Definition

Events affecting strategic minerals needed for technology, energy transition, defense, batteries, or advanced manufacturing.

### Inclusion Criteria

- Export restrictions on critical minerals.
- Mining nationalization.
- Processing capacity policy.
- Strategic stockpile action.
- Subsidy or investment program for critical minerals.

### Exclusion Criteria

- Routine commodity price movements.
- Mining-company operational updates without geopolitical or policy relevance.

### Example Events

- Export restriction on battery minerals.
- Strategic minerals processing subsidy.
- Mine nationalization tied to geopolitical leverage.

## Cyber Operations

### Definition

State-linked or geopolitically significant cyber activity affecting infrastructure, firms, government systems, or strategic sectors.

### Inclusion Criteria

- Publicly attributed state-linked cyber operations.
- Infrastructure cyberattack with geopolitical relevance.
- Sanctions or restrictions following cyber activity.
- Major data or IP theft linked to strategic competition.

### Exclusion Criteria

- Routine criminal cyber incidents without geopolitical attribution.
- Vulnerability disclosures without event impact.

### Example Events

- State-linked intrusion disclosure.
- Cyberattack on energy infrastructure.
- Sanctions after cyber operation attribution.

## Defense Industrial Policy

### Definition

Government policy, procurement, investment, or production action affecting defense-industrial capacity and strategic security supply chains.

### Inclusion Criteria

- Defense-production funding.
- Munitions capacity expansion.
- Procurement restrictions.
- Defense export approvals or blocks.
- Strategic supply-chain support for defense inputs.

### Exclusion Criteria

- Military exercises without industrial-policy component.
- Company contract wins with no policy or geopolitical significance.

### Example Events

- Munitions production expansion funding.
- Defense procurement restriction.
- Strategic electronics funding for defense supply chains.

## AI Infrastructure Competition

### Definition

Events affecting the geopolitical competition for AI compute, cloud access, data centers, accelerators, power availability, or AI infrastructure supply chains.

### Inclusion Criteria

- AI-chip restrictions.
- Compute or cloud access controls.
- Data-center investment policy.
- Accelerator subsidy programs.
- AI infrastructure export or licensing controls.

### Exclusion Criteria

- General AI product launches.
- Model releases without geopolitical infrastructure or policy relevance.

### Example Events

- Cloud access restriction for strategic competitors.
- AI data-center infrastructure policy.
- Accelerator subsidy or export-control rule.
