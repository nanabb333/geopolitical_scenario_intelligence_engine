# Product Demo Walkthrough

This walkthrough shows how an analyst would use the question-centric workflow. It uses a supported export-control example and does not invent unsupported results.

## Question

```text
What happened historically when advanced semiconductor export controls expanded?
```

## Scenario Family

Primary scenario family: Technology Competition.

Related scenario family: Strategic Competition.

Rationale: The question concerns export controls, advanced semiconductors, and US-China strategic competition mechanisms.

## Historical Analogues

Relevant analogue types:

- BIS advanced computing and semiconductor manufacturing export controls.
- BIS updates advanced computing and semiconductor export controls.
- Semiconductor-equipment export controls.
- Entity-list actions where technology restriction is the mechanism.

The workbench should prioritize policy mechanism, sector exposure, geography, and coding notes. Analogues are historical context, not predictions.

## Market Evidence

Market evidence exists in the V2 market reaction pilot for selected export-control and AI-chip restriction events.

Relevant evidence types:

- sector proxy rows;
- named exposed equity rows where defensible;
- benchmark comparison rows;
- short event windows such as `t0` and `m3_p3`.

The analyst should disclose that the V2 market layer is a pilot and is not yet fully integrated into the V1 workbench.

## Observed Pathways

Likely pathway frame: restriction pathway.

Potential analyst considerations:

- directly constrained firms;
- substitute beneficiaries;
- semiconductor equipment exposure;
- supply-chain rerouting;
- policy implementation timing;
- anticipation before announcement.

These are pathway frames, not forecasts.

## Current Relevance

Current relevance should compare:

- historical similarity: does the new question resemble prior export-control events?
- structural similarity: is it sector-wide or named-firm specific?
- policy similarity: does the mechanism match prior export controls?
- sector exposure: are AI chips, semiconductor equipment, or foundries involved?
- market evidence: are there comparable market rows, and what are the proxy limitations?

## Executive Memo

The executive memo should include:

- concise scenario classification;
- closest historical analogues;
- available market evidence;
- restriction pathway interpretation;
- caveats on proxy selection and event-window confounds;
- unsupported claims that should be avoided.

The memo must not include forecasts, probabilities, expected returns, trading signals, or investment advice.
